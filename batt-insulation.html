What is Batt Insulation?

Batts are a form of insulation that are pre-cut in flat pieces. They resemble a small blanket. Most common forms of insulation come in batts – fiberglass, mineral wool, sheep’s wool. Typically batts come in bags and they’re sized to fit into standard cavities. Batt widths vary from 3.5 inches (for a 2×4 cavity) to 5.5 inches (for a 2×6 cavity), though they can be thicker. The batt length is normally 48 inches though sometimes you’ll see 96 inches. Manufacturers will note how many square feet each bag of batts covers.

What is the R-Value of Batt Insulation?

R-Value refers to the thermal conductivity of insulation. The higher the R-Value, the better it insulates. The R-Values of batts depends on the material used and thickness of the product so you will see a wide range. When looking at R-Value, we like to assess on R-Value per inch so you can compare on thermal conductivity, not the amount of insulation used. A higher R-Value per inch requires less insulation to achieve the same overall R-Value. The R-Value per inch for batt insulation ranges from 2.1 to 3.6. Note this is generally less than what you’ll see with various forms of loose-fill insulation.

The Benefits of Batt Insulation

The main benefit of batt insulation is ease of install. You can specify batt dimensions for your project making the installation process easy, whether you are a professional or DIY’er. Batts also insulate well, as measured by R-Value.

Batt insulation offers excellent thermal resistance, reducing the workload on your heating and air conditioning systems. Less stress on your HVAC unit means less energy being used and lower energy bills. According to ENERGY STAR, properly insulating and sealing your home can lead to an average of 15% savings on your heating and cooling costs per year. Ultimately, your insulation pays for itself over time through your energy savings.

Installing batt insulation, along with air sealing, helps your home maintain stable indoor temperatures, keeping you comfortable all year long. In fact, the Department of Energy promotes the installation of insulation and air sealing as one of the fastest ways to increase your home’s comfort.

Fiberglass batt insulationis highly effective at absorbing sound waves, reducing noise between rooms and from the outside, creating a more peaceful home environment for you and your family or future residents.

When combined with air sealing, batt insulation helps limit outside moisture from entering walls, reducing the risk of condensation and mold growth. It also helps keep pollen, dust, and insects out of your home.

It’s great to know that batt insulation is also one of the most cost-effective insulation solutions for your home or building project. It’s relatively affordable and easy to install, making it a great option for DIYers (although professional installation is always preferred to ensure proper installation and effectiveness). Your upfront costs are often paid back by your energy savings in only a few short years.

Insulation can be one of the most cost-effective ways to improve your comfort and energy bill.
